China's Ministry of Industry and Information Technology put hard numbers behind the country's humanoid lead on **July 20, 2026**. [Qazinform](https://qazinform.com/news/china-leads-global-humanoid-robot-development-cfdd58), citing **Xinhua**, and [Global Times](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202607/1366371.shtml) both report the same MIIT readout: China has developed **more than 400 complete humanoid robot products**, accounting for **over half** of the global total. Chinese-made **quadrupeds** also took **nearly 70%** of worldwide sales in the **first half of 2026**.

That is a stock-of-models claim for humanoids, not a promise that 400 new living-room bots shipped this year. For HomeBotRadar readers, it explains why so many of the platforms we track keep coming out of Shenzhen, Hangzhou, and Beijing labs first.

## What MIIT and customs added

[Global Times](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202607/1366371.shtml) nested the humanoid and quadruped figures in a broader tech briefing:

- **Humanoids:** 400-plus complete products, more than half of world supply (MIIT)
- **Quadrupeds:** nearly **70%** of global sales in H1 2026 (MIIT)
- **Zhejiang exports:** robot exports topped **1 billion yuan (~$147 million)** in the first half; intelligent bionic robot exports were about **60%** of China's national total (Hangzhou Customs)
- **Hangzhou cluster:** more than **700** upstream and downstream robot firms in the city alone

The timing sat next to the **2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference** in Shanghai, where humanoids were on the show floor and Kazakhstan's president attended the opening, per Qazinform's earlier WAIC coverage.

[TechNode](https://technode.com/2026/07/20/china-develops-more-than-400-humanoid-robot-products-accounting-for-over-half-of-the-global-total/) pointed readers back to the Chinese **Xinhua** wire for the same MIIT data. We are not treating "400 models" as 400 buyable home robots. Many will be research prototypes, industrial demos, or short-run SKUs that never reach a consumer storefront.

## How this shows up in our catalog

HomeBotRadar already lists several China-rooted humanoids and companions shoppers actually compare:
