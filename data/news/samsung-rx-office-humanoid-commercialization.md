[Korea JoongAng Daily](https://www.koreajoongangdaily.com/business/samsung-creates-roboticsdedicated-unit-under-ceo/12784702) reports that **Samsung Electronics** has stood up a dedicated **Robotics eXperience (RX)** office under **Roh Tae-moon**, president and chief of the **Device eXperience (DX)** division that already runs smartphones and home appliances. The goal is clear: stop treating robotics as scattered R&D and push **humanoid commercialization**.

The story landed July 21 in Korean coverage and is the one we are filing for HomeBotRadar readers on **July 23, 2026**. For shoppers who still remember **Ballie**, Samsung's spherical home companion that we track as a shelved prototype, this is the organizational bet behind whatever comes next.

## What changed inside Samsung

According to JoongAng Daily, RX pulls together strategy, product planning, and hardware/software development that used to sit in separate pockets. The move builds on Samsung's **2024** acquisition of **Rainbow Robotics** and a companywide staffing push for the new unit.

Key people named in the report:

- **Lee Dong-geun**, executive vice president and head of the robotics strategy team (joined in May after leading robotics strategy for **Boston Dynamics** at Hyundai Motor Group)
- **Oh Jun-ho** (KAIST), still leading the Future Robotics Office
- **Kim Hyoun-jin** (Seoul National University), autonomous robots and control, into RX
- **Kim Ui-kyum** (Ajou University), robotic hand specialist, also into RX

Samsung is turning its **Seoul R&D Campus** in Seocho into the main robotics research hub (two dedicated buildings) and plans a **data factory** at its **Gumi** complex to train robots at scale, plus more global research hubs.

## Factory first, living room later

JoongAng Daily says Samsung wants humanoids on its own **semiconductor, smartphone, and home appliance** lines as early as the **second half of 2026**, mainly to collect real-world data on dexterity. Roh said in January that manufacturing automation is the top priority, then a step-by-step move from industrial B2B into a broader consumer market.

That roadmap matters for HomeBotRadar. It matches what we already see elsewhere: **Figure 02**, **Unitree G1**, and Hyundai's **Boston Dynamics** stack are still factory and logistics stories first. Samsung is also accelerating robotic hands and claims in-house work on handling flexible, cable-like materials that keep changing shape.

If RX proves production lines and a path to profit, the report says the unit could later graduate into a full business division. Samsung also flagged more robotics M&A and strategic investment at its Q1 shareholder presentation.

## What this means for HomeBotRadar

This is an **org and roadmap story**, not a new consumer SKU. We are **not** adding a Samsung humanoid to the [catalog](/robots) yet, and we are **not** changing **Ballie** scores just because RX exists.
